Bio

As a teenager, I can recall completing a career assessment which indicated I should become a counselor. That seemed reasonable - make my own hours, listen to people talk, what wouldn’t there be to love about that? But, as you know, life happens and I became an angsty teenager, listening to punk rock, dreaming of writing articles about bands and traveling the world. That didn’t quite pan out, but amid my youth, I found myself eagerly wanting to help others. Whether that be listening to my friend’s issues and providing support and encouragement, or helping peers with disabilities, I found myself drawn to those struggling and searching for answers.

I experienced my first counseling session in college. Life was changing quickly, and I was unclear about where I was heading. When I sat down with the therapist, I no longer needed to speak about career goals or the present, but rather the weight of past events I had been carrying with me for decades. It was cathartic and finally gave me a sense of peace. It was terrifying, being vulnerable with a stranger, but I trusted her and was confident she would provide a safe space for me to be myself and tell my story. That is my hope for you. I want you to tell your story and not be afraid of judgement. There is no judgement here.

Fast-forward and here we are. A mother of 3 young kids and a wife, who works full-time. A professional attempting to expand her career and attain personal and professional goals. In this season of life, I am keenly aware of the struggle’s parents face with balancing work/life and the need to remember who we are outside of mom/dad/teacher. As a parent of a child with a congenital heart condition, I am familiar with the need for support to adjust to a life not previously imagined. I have grieved the life I thought I would have and accepted the life I do have. I have come to view the challenges as just that, challenges which aid us to grow and thrive.

My counseling style and strategies have been uniquely shaped by life experiences and each professional position I have held. Having worked in higher education, non-profit, private rehabilitation, and currently as a Vocational Rehabilitation Counselor with TWC, I have accumulated a wealth of knowledge and skills to serve a variety of individuals. While each client’s sessions are individualized, I tend to rely on Cognitive Behavioral, Reality, Solution-Focused, and Person-Centered therapies. I believe a client should establish goals and my role is to assist in achieving them. I am cognizant of multicultural issues and welcome clients from all races, ethnicities, cultural backgrounds, and sexual orientations. I am open-minded and an ally to those who feel marginalized, discriminated against or forgotten. While I may not understand what life is like in your shoes, I do have an open-mind and heart to hear your story and support you moving forward.
